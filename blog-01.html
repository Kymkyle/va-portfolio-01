10 Tasks You Can Outsource to a Virtual Assistant to Save Hours Every Week

By: Karen Young

Are you drowning in to-dos and feeling like there aren’t enough hours in the day? If you're an entrepreneur, coach, or small business owner, you’ve likely hit that wall. The good news? You don’t have to do it all yourself.
Hiring a Virtual Assistant (VA) is one of the smartest moves you can make to free up your time, reduce stress, and grow your business more efficiently. Here are 10 everyday tasks you can hand off to a VA — and instantly reclaim hours of your week.
1. Email Management:
Tired of sorting through hundreds of emails? A VA can:
  • Organize your inbox
  • Filter out spam
  • Draft responses
This keeps your communications professional and your inbox under control.
2. Calendar & Appointment Scheduling
Avoid back-and-forth emails and double bookings. Your VA can:
  • Manage your calendar
  • Schedule meetings
  • Send reminders
  • Block focus time
Your day flows smoothly, and you stay focused.
3. Social Media Scheduling
Stay visible online without spending hours creating content. A VA can:
  • Draft and schedule posts
  • Respond to comments/messages
  • Research hashtags
  • Create basic graphics using tools like Canva
It’s consistent marketing — without the time drain.
4. Online Research & Data Entry
Need info on competitors, clients, or vendors? A VA can:
  • Collect and summarize research
  • Input data into spreadsheets or CRMs
  • Keep everything organized and accessible
Ideal for market research or lead generation.
5. Customer Service Support
Your VA can handle:
  • Customer inquiries via email or live chat
  • Order follow-ups
  • FAQ responses
Happy clients = stronger relationships and repeat business.
6. Travel Planning
Planning a trip? Let a VA:
  • Research flights and hotels
  • Compare prices
  • Create itineraries
All you have to do is pack!
7. Document Creation & Formatting
A VA can help you create:
  • Reports
  • Presentations
  • Proposals
  • E-books or PDF guides
Everything looks polished and professional.
8. CRM Updates
Keep your customer relationship management system clean and current. A VA can:
  • Add new leads
  • Update contact info
  • Track touchpoints
No more losing track of potential clients or follow-ups.
9. Invoicing & Payment Tracking
Avoid payment delays and messy books. Your VA can:
  • Send invoices
  • Track due dates
  • Follow up on late payments
It keeps your cash flow healthy.
10. Personal Errands
Yes, your VA can even help with personal tasks like:
  • Booking appointments
  • Ordering gifts
  • Sending thank-you cards
Because your time outside work matters, too.
